The bridge

Fuel poverty is a retrofit signal

A home only counts as fuel-poor if its efficiency sits in band D–G — a hard gate in England's LILEE metric. So predicting fuel-poverty risk surfaces exactly the leaky, low-efficiency stock that a retrofit exists to fix.

The link

Fuel-poor → inefficient

By definition a fuel-poor household lives in a band D–G property. Rank homes by fuel-poverty risk and you are, in effect, ranking them by fabric inefficiency — the raw material of a retrofit programme.

The payoff

Inefficient → decarbonise

Those same homes burn the most fuel to stay warm. Retrofitting their fabric is where each pound removes the most carbon — which is exactly what the brief asks us to find.

This is why a fuel-poverty model answers a decarbonisation question — both point at the same low-efficiency buildings. Income helps sharpen the social-housing priority; the fabric is what drives the carbon saving.

Two feature sets, kept disjoint

Method

A synthetic target, then a leak-guarded model

No building carries a ground-truth risk label, so we generate one. Aggregate LILEE probabilities become priors for a Bayesian MCMC simulation that assigns every home a fuel-poverty risk band. Machine-learning models then learn to predict that band from the separate modelling feature set.

STEP A

Priors from LILEE

Local-authority fuel-poverty rates seed the model — the strongest public signal of where risk concentrates.

STEP B

MCMC synthetic target

A Bayesian simulation, run to convergence at 1,000 iterations, assigns each home a risk band: Less Likely → Highly Likely.

STEP C

Predict & guard the leak

XGBoost, Random Forest, DNN and multinomial LR predict the band — each trained twice, with and without current energy efficiency (Pipeline A / B), to isolate any leakage.

The MCMC feature set and the ML modelling set are disjoint by design — "carefully avoiding possible signal leak."

Way forward

From 10k rows to all of London

⚙️

Scale the compute

We modelled 10,000 of 2.6M collected rows on limited hardware. Distributed training unlocks the full stock and the 23-way LILEE supplementary segments we had to leave out.

🔬

MCMC-Bayesian extension

Prior studies stop at local-authority-level probability. Our Markov-Chain Monte Carlo prior pushes prediction to the building level from a different feature set — a genuinely novel step, kept as a research track.
